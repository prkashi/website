What is Chess?

Chess is a Game not a Sports

Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on a chessboard, a checkered game board with 64 squares arranged in an eight-by-eight grid. Chess is played by millions of people worldwide, both amateurs and professionals.

A brief history of your favorite game

Chess was invented around 500 A.D. in India, as a game for four people - Chaturanga. It is said that an Indian ambassador brought the game as a present to his king to the Persian court of Chosarus. When Persia was invaded by Arab, the chess culture was taken to Arab. Even back then in Arab the game fascinated all those who came into contact with it. Via the Arabs the game of chess spread as far as Europe. Via Spain and Italy it reached the countries of south , central and western Europe and the game came to Russia through Caucasus. There soon arose a literature with chess puzzles and even already professional players. Since the 15th century chess articles have also been published in Europe. At present, number of books published on chess is estimated at over 90000 titles. If you are a collector of books, there you go, you have a mountain to climb. There were soon especially smart players who astounded spectators with their skills. Unlike in many other games or sports, it is easy to find out who is best: in a game or a match between them.
